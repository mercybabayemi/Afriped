GENERATIONS OF COMPUTER
The computer presently has five generations; each generation marks a significant leap in computing technology, often characterized by changes in hardware and software.
First Generation (1940s - 1950s)
Technology: Vacuum Tubes
Examples: ENIAC, EDVAC, EDSAC, UNIVAC.
Characteristics:
Computers used vacuum tubes for circuitry and magnetic drums for memory.
These computers were enormous.
Second Generation (1950s - 1960s)
Technology: Transistors
Examples: IBM 7090, UNIVAC II, ATLAS
Characteristics:
Transistors replaced vacuum tubes, making computers smaller and more energy-efficient.
Magnetic cores were used/
Third Generation (1960s - 1970s)
Technology: Integrated Circuits (ICs)
Examples: IBM 360 series, PDP-8.
Characteristics:
Integrated Circuits (ICs) combined multiple transistors.
Computers became more powerful and were able to support multiple users.

GENERATION OF COMPUTER (Cont’d)
Fourth Generation (1970s - Present)
Technology: Microprocessors
Examples: Intel 4004 (first microprocessor), Apple Macintosh, IBM PC.
Characteristics:
Computers became even smaller, more powerful, and more affordable.
Networking and the development of the Internet.
Fifth Generation (Present and Beyond)
Technology: Artificial Intelligence (AI)
Examples: IBM Watson (AI), Google Quantum Computer.
Characteristics:
These computers can process natural language, recognize patterns, and solve complex problems much faster than traditional computers.
Perform tasks using natural human interaction.
