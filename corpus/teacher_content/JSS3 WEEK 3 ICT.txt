DIGITAL DIVIDE
The digital divide refers to the gap between individuals who have access to modern information and communication technology (ICT) and those with little or no access at all.
FACTORS CONTRIBUTING TO THE DIGITAL DIVIDE
Economic Barriers: Inability to afford devices, internet services, or other technology-related expenses.
Geographical Barriers: Remote and rural areas often lack infrastructure, such as broadband networks.
Educational Barriers: People with less education may lack the skills necessary to use digital tools effectively.
Social Barriers: Age, gender, and disability can affect access to and use of technology.
Policy and Infrastructure: Lack of government investment in technology infrastructure and education widens the divide
IMPACTS OF THE DIGITAL DIVIDE
Educational Inequality: Students without internet access or digital skills face difficulties keeping up with modern learning methods.
Economic Disparity: Lack of access to technology limits job opportunities, entrepreneurship, and digital innovation.
Social Isolation: Those without access to technology are cut off from important communication
Civic Participation: People may not be able to engage in e-governance or access essential online services.
OLD ECONOMY AND NEW ECONOMY
OLD ECONOMY
The Old Economy refers to the business or economic system of the pre-technological period. It is characterized by manual labor and face-to-face service delivery.
FEATURES OF THE OLD ECONOMY
It was time consuming
It was labour based
It was mechanical
It was constrained by distance
It was constrained by space
It was constrained by time
LIMITATIONS OF THE OLD ECONOMY
Space: Dependent on physical locations like markets and offices for transactions.
Distance: Business required physical presence, making long-distance transactions difficult.
Time: Transactions were limited to specific hours, unlike 24/7 availability in the new economy.
Technology: Minimal use of technology made operations labor-intensive and inefficient.
