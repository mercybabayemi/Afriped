LOGIC GATES
A logic gate is a basic component of digital circuits, designed to perform logical operations. It takes one or more binary inputs and produces a single binary output.
They rely on Boolean algebra to process logical operations and are essential in the functioning of digital systems such as computers, phones, and memory devices.
TYPES OF LOGIC GATES
The logic gates can be classified into the following major types:
Basic Logic Gates: - There are three basic logic gates:
AND Gate
OR Gate
NOT Gate
Universal/Alternate Logic Gates: - In digital electronics, the following two logic gates are considered as universal logic gates:
NOR Gate
NAND Gate
Derived Logic Gates: - The following two are the derived logic gates used in digital systems:
XOR Gate
XNOR Gate

Uses of Logic Gates
Logic gates are used to identify high and low voltage points in circuits.
They assist technicians and engineers in determining and verifying output levels.
Logic gates are crucial for designing electrical pathways in electronic components.
They play a key role in decision-making processes by providing yes or no responses based on inputs.
Logic gates are essential in constructing alarm systems for homes and vehicles.
