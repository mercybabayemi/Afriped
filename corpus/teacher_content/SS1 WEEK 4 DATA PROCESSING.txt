CLASSIFICATION BASED ON SIZE
There are four major sizes of computers, super, mainframe, mini and microcomputers.
Supercomputers: The most powerful computers, designed for complex computations and processing large datasets. Used in scientific research, climate modeling, and simulations. Supercomputers are the largest, fastest and most expensive computers.
Mainframe Computers: Large, powerful systems used by large organizations for bulk data processing, such as transaction processing and managing large databases. It has a very large memory size but less than that of supercomputers.
Minicomputers: Mid-sized computers that are less powerful than mainframes but still capable of supporting multiple users simultaneously. Often used in business applications. They allow users to share access to the hardware through stations called TERMINALS.
Microcomputers: Commonly known as personal computers (PCs), these are designed for individual use. They include desktops, laptops, tablets, and smartphones.
