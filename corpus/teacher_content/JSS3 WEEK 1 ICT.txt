Definition of the Internet
The internet is a global network of interconnected computers that allows users to share and access information, communicate with others, and perform a variety of tasks.
Basic Terms Associated with the Internet
World Wide Web (WWW): A collection of web pages that are accessed via the internet.
Browser: A software application used to access and navigate the World Wide Web. Examples include Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Safari, and Microsoft Edge.
URL (Uniform Resource Locator): The address used to access a specific resource on the internet, such as a website. For example, `https://www.google.com` is a URL.
Search Engine: A tool that helps users find information on the internet by entering keywords or phrases. Examples are Google, Bing, and Yahoo.
Website: This is a collection of related web pages that are hosted on one web server.
Email (Electronic mail):  A method of exchanging digital messages over the internet.
Wi-Fi (Wireless Fidelity): A technology that allows devices to connect to the internet wirelessly within a certain range.
IP (Internet Protocol) Address: A unique string of numbers separated by periods or colons that identifies each device connected to the internet.
Download: Downloading refers to the process of receiving data from the internet or another computer to your own device.
Upload: It involves sending or transferring data from your device to the internet or another computer

Uses of the Internet
Communication: Email, social media, and messaging apps help people stay in touch no matter where they are.
Education: The internet is a valuable resource for learning. You can find information, watch educational videos, and even attend online classes.
Entertainment: Streaming movies, playing games, and listening to music are popular ways to enjoy the internet.
Shopping: Online stores allow you to buy almost anything and have it delivered to your door.
Research: Whether for school projects or personal interests, the internet offers vast amounts of information on almost any topic.
Advantages of the Internet
Access to Information: Instant access to a wealth of knowledge.
Global Communication: Easily connect with people worldwide.
Education: Online learning opportunities and resources.
Convenience: Online shopping, banking, and services.
Entertainment: Streaming, gaming, and social media.
Economic Growth: Creates new business and job opportunities.
Cultural Exchange: Enables global sharing of ideas and traditions.
