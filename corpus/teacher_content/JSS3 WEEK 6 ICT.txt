SPREADSHEET FEATURES AND TERMINOLOGIES
Column: A vertical range of cells labeled with letters (e.g., A, B, C).
Row: A horizontal range of cells labeled with numbers (e.g., 1, 2, 3).
Cell: The intersection of a row and a column, labeled by its column letter and row number (e.g., C3).
Cell Address: The name of a cell (e.g., C3), identifying its position by column and row.
Range: A group of selected cells, either adjacent or non-adjacent (e.g., A1).
Worksheet: A single page within a workbook containing rows, columns, and cells.
Workbook: A file containing multiple worksheets, often with links between them.
Formula: A calculation or instruction to manipulate data in cells (e.g., +B1/B4).
Function: A pre-defined formula for complex calculations (e.g., =SUM(B1)).
HOW TO LOAD AND EXIT THE SPREADSHEET PACKAGE.
To load a spreadsheet package like Microsoft Excel, you can follow these steps:
Click on the Start button located at the bottom-left corner of the screen.
Type Microsoft Excel in the search bar.
Click on Microsoft Excel to launch the application.
To exit a spreadsheet package like Microsoft Excel, you can follow these steps:
Simply click the (X) button on the top right corner of your spreadsheet window.
