PRESENTATION PACKAGES
A presentation package is a software application used to create and display information in the form of a slideshow. It produces visual aids that are often used for educational, business, or informational purposes, aiming to effectively convey ideas to an audience.
EXAMPLES OF PRESENTATION PACKAGES:
Microsoft PowerPoint
Macromedia Flash
Windows Movie Maker
Google Slides
Apple Keynote
Prezi
Canva
Adobe Persuasion
Corel Presentation
Harvard Graphics
FEATURES OF A PRESENTATION PACKAGE
Slide Creation: Organize content using slides, with pre-designed templates and customizable layouts.
Multimedia Insertion: Add pictures, videos, and audio to enhance presentations.
Animations and Transitions: Apply object animations and slide transitions for a dynamic presentation.
Slideshow Mode: Preview and deliver presentations with slideshow and presenter view options.
Graphics and Charts: Create shapes and charts (e.g., pie charts, bar graphs) to visualize data.
Text Formatting: Customize fonts, add bullet points, and control text appearance with animations.
Collaboration and Sharing: Enable real-time collaboration and share presentations via cloud services.
Exporting and Cloud Integration: Save presentations in formats like PDF or video, with cloud storage access.

GETTING STARTED WITH POWERPOINT
Opening PowerPoint:
Start > All Programs > Microsoft Office > PowerPoint.
Create a New Presentation:
Open PowerPoint and select Blank Presentation.
Choose the Title Slide layout.
Adding Content:
Text: Click the title or subtitle areas to type.
Graphics: Insert images or shapes via the Insert menu.
Animating Content:
Add motion effects to slides for dynamic presentations.
Adding New Slides:
Go to Home > New Slide and choose a layout.
Saving the Presentation:
Click File > Save As or Ctrl+S
Choose a location and enter the file name.
Running a Slide Show:
Press F5 or click the Slide Show tab > From Beginning.
ANIMATIONS AND TRANSITIONS.
Animation: This refers to effects applied to individual elements within a slide, such as text, images, or shapes. Animations can make elements appear, disappear, or move in various ways, helping to emphasize or draw attention to specific content.
Transition: This refers to the visual effects that occur between slides. Transitions control how one slide moves to the next, they help to create a smooth flow between different sections of your presentation.
