UNITS OF STORAGE
Memory storage is measured in various units, including:
Binary Digit (BIT): This refers to the smallest basic unit of information that can be stored on a machine. It exists in one of two possible distinct states(0 or 1).
NIBBLE: In computing, a nibble is a four-bit aggregation, or half a byte.
Byte (B): The basic unit of digital data, consisting of 8 bits.
Size: 1 byte = 8 bits.
Kilobyte (KB): A unit of digital information storage equal to 1,024 bytes.
Size: 1 KB = 1,024 bytes.
Megabyte (MB): A unit of digital information storage equal to 1,024 kilobytes.
Size: 1 MB = 1,024 KB or 1,048,576 bytes.
Gigabyte (GB): A unit of digital information storage equal to 1,024 megabytes.
Size: 1 GB = 1,024 MB or 1,073,741,824 bytes.
Terabyte (TB): A unit of digital information storage equal to 1,024 gigabytes.
Size: 1 TB = 1,024 GB or 1,099,511,627,776 bytes.

NOTE:
To convert from larger to smaller units, multiply
To convert from smaller to larger units, divide
