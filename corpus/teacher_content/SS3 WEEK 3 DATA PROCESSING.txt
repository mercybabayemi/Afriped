DATABASE SECURITY
Database Security refers to the protective measures implemented to safeguard a database against unauthorized access, threats, and vulnerabilities. It involves ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data stored within the system. This can be achieved through various methods, such as encryption, firewalls, authentication, and access control.
Objectives of Database Security
1. Confidentiality: Ensuring that only authorized users can access sensitive information.
2. Integrity: Ensuring the accuracy and reliability of the data.
3. Availability: Ensuring that the database and its resources are accessible to authorized users whenever needed.
ACCESS CONTROL
Access Control is a crucial aspect of database security that determines who can access and manipulate specific resources within the database. It involves defining permissions and restrictions for users to ensure that sensitive information is only available to those with the proper authorization.
Types of Access Control:
1. Discretionary Access Control (DAC):
- In DAC, the owner of the data or administrator defines who can access the data and what operations they can perform (read, write, execute).
- It is based on the identity of the user or group.
2. Mandatory Access Control (MAC)
- In MAC, access permissions are determined by the system according to policies defined by the administrator, not by the users.
- Typically used in environments requiring high security, such as government databases.
3. Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
- In RBAC, access is based on the roles assigned to users within the organization.
- Permissions are granted based on roles rather than individual users, making it easier to manage access rights for a large number of users.

Key Components of Access Control:
1. Authentication: Verifying the identity of the user before granting access.
2. Authorization: Granting permissions to authenticated users to perform specific actions on the database.
3.Privileges: Specific rights or permissions assigned to users or roles, such as the ability to read, write, or delete data.
Benefits of Access Control
- Prevents unauthorized access to sensitive data.
- Reduces the risk of data breaches by limiting access to critical resources.
- Ensures compliance with regulations and organizational policies.
