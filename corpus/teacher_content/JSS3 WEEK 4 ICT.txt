DIGITAL LITERACY
Digital literacy refers to the ability to effectively use digital tools, technology, and online resources to locate, evaluate, create, and communicate information.
CHARACTERISTICS OF DIGITAL LITERACY
Proficient in using devices like computers, smartphones, and tablets.
Capable of evaluating the credibility of online information.
Knowledgeable in digital security, privacy, and online etiquette.
Skilled in creating digital content such as blogs, videos, and presentations.
Proficient in using email, social media, and other digital communication platforms.
FEATURES OF DIGITAL LITERACY
Adaptability: Adjusting to new digital tools and platforms.
Ethical Use: Using technology responsibly and ethically.
Problem Solving: Troubleshooting and resolving digital issues.
Collaboration: Engaging effectively in digital teamwork.
Life-Long Learning: Continuously updating digital skills.
Online Safety and Privacy: Protecting personal data and understanding security.
Effective Use of Tools: Proficiency in using digital tools.
Critical Evaluation: Assessing the credibility of online information.
Digital Communication Etiquette: Communicating appropriately online.

SCOPE OF DIGITAL LITERACY
Education: Incorporating technology into learning and teaching.
Workplace: Using digital tools for productivity and collaboration.
Communication: Effective use of digital platforms to share information.
Civic Engagement: Participation in online communities, and staying informed on digital platforms.
Digital Content Creation: Making and distributing content online.
DIGITAL LITERACY IN EDUCATION
Schools are incorporating digital literacy into the curriculum to keep up with technological advances. This includes using computers, educational software, and online course materials.
Classrooms may feature smart boards and audience response systems, which are most effective when teachers are digitally literate.
Teachers also instruct students on verifying credible online sources and preventing plagiarism.
DIGITAL LITERACY IN THE WORKFORCE
Many jobs now require digital literacy, including proficiency in using computers, smartphones, and the internet. Certain roles demand proof of digital literacy for hiring or promotion.
Employers, especially in fields like manufacturing, retail, and construction, rely on digital tools to boost efficiency and safety.
Digital literacy is crucial for job seekers, as recruiters increasingly use online platforms to find candidates.
