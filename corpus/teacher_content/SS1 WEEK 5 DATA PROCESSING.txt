CLASSIFICATION BASED ON FUNCTIONALITY
There are three types depending on the way they present information such as analog, digital and hybrid computers.
Analog Computers: These computers process data in continuous form, typically used for scientific and engineering purposes. Examples include early mechanical devices like the slide rule, car speedometer, rotating clock.
Digital Computers: Most modern computers fall into this category, processing data in binary form or discrete numbers of decimal. They include all types of PCs, servers, GSM, phone, digital radio, etc.
Hybrid Computers: Combining features of both analog and digital computers, hybrid computers can process both continuous and discrete data. They are often used in specialized fields like medical equipment.

CLASSIFICATION BASED ON PURPOSE
Computers are made for either two purposes, namely, special or general purpose.
General-Purpose Computers: Designed to perform a variety of tasks. These are the most common computers, including desktops, laptops, and servers.
Special-Purpose Computers: Built for a specific task or application. Examples include embedded systems in devices like ATMs, GPS units, or medical equipment.
CLASSIFICATION BASED ON GENERATION
The computer presently has five generations; each generation marks a significant leap in computing technology, often characterized by changes in hardware and software.
