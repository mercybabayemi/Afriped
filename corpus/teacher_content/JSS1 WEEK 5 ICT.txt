EVOLUTION OF ICT
ICT (Information and Communication Technology): The use of computers and other electronic communication means to manage and process information effectively.
The evolution of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) can be broken down into various stages, with key inventions marking significant changes. The stages of ICT in order of their dates include:
Invention of printing
Invention of Telephone
Invention of Radio
Invention of Television
Invention of Computer

EVOLUTION OF ICT
Printing (1440): The invention of the printing press by Johannes Gutenberg revolutionized the distribution of information, allowing books and documents to be produced quickly and efficiently.
Telephone (1876): Invented by Alexander Graham Bell, the telephone enabled voice communication over long distances, marking a significant step in communication technology.
Radio (1895): Radio waves, first used for wireless telegraphy and later for audio broadcasting, allowed for mass communication and became one of the first widely used wireless communication technologies. Invented by Guglielmo Marconi.
