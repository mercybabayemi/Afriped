CLASSIFICATIONS OF COMPUTERS
A computer is an electronic device, which accepts data as an input, processed the input (data) and produces an output called information.
Computers can be classified based on various criteria. These include the following:
Classification based on size.
Classification based on Functionality.
Classification based on purpose.
Classification based on generation.
CLASSIFICATION BASED ON SIZE
There are four major sizes of computers, super, mainframe, mini and microcomputers.
Supercomputers: The most powerful computers, designed for complex computations and processing large datasets. Used in scientific research, climate modeling, and simulations. Supercomputers are the largest, fastest and most expensive computers.
Mainframe Computers: Large, powerful systems used by large organizations for bulk data processing, such as transaction processing and managing large databases. It has a very large memory size but less than that of supercomputers.
Minicomputers: Mid-sized computers that are less powerful than mainframes but still capable of supporting multiple users simultaneously. Often used in business applications. They allow users to share access to the hardware through stations called TERMINALS.
Microcomputers: Commonly known as personal computers (PCs), these are designed for individual use. They include desktops, laptops, tablets, and smartphones.

CLASSIFICATION OF COMPUTERS
CLASSIFICATION BASED ON FUNCTIONALITY
There are three types depending on the way they present information such as analog, digital and hybrid computers.
Analog Computers: These computers process data in continuous form, typically used for scientific and engineering purposes. Examples include early mechanical devices like the slide rule, car speedometer, rotating clock.
Digital Computers: Most modern computers fall into this category, processing data in binary form or discrete numbers of decimal. They include all types of PCs, servers, GSM, phone, digital radio, etc.
Hybrid Computers: Combining features of both analog and digital computers, hybrid computers can process both continuous and discrete data. They are often used in specialized fields like medical equipment.
CLASSIFICATION BASED ON PURPOSE
Computer can be made for either two purposes, namely, special or general purpose.
General-Purpose Computers: Designed to perform a variety of tasks. These are the most common computers, including desktops, laptops, and servers.
Special-Purpose Computers: Built for a specific task or application. Examples include embedded systems in devices like ATMs, GPS units, or medical equipment.
