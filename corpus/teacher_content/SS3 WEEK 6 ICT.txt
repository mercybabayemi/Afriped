HIGH-LEVEL LANGUAGE (HLL)
A High-Level Language (HLL) is a computer programming language that is machine-independent and designed to solve problems by reflecting the requirements of a task.
HLLs resemble natural language or mathematical notation, making them easier to read, write, and maintain compared to low-level languages.
They require translators (compilers or interpreters) to convert their instructions into machine language.
Examples of High Level Language
BASIC, FORTRAN, ALGOL, PASCAL, LISP, COBOL, Python, Java, C++, JavaScript, Ruby, PHP, Swift, etc.
CLASSIFICATION OF HIGH-LEVEL LANGUAGES
By Purpose
HLLs are categorized based on their intended application:
General Purpose HLL: These languages are not designed for a specific problem or application and can be used across various domains. Examples include: C, Java, BASIC, PASCAL, PL/I.
Special Purpose HLL: These languages are designed to address specific types of problems:
Scientific HLL: Used for solving scientific problems, e.g., BASIC, FORTRAN, ALGOL.
Commercial/Business HLL: Designed for business or commercial tasks, e.g., COBOL.
Artificial Intelligence HLL: Used for AI and robotics, e.g., LISP, PROLOG.
By Method of Translation
HLLs can also be classified based on how they are translated into machine language:
Interpreted Languages: These use an interpreter to convert code into machine language line-by-line during execution. Examples include BASIC, LISP, Python, Java, Ruby, Perl, MATLAB, PASCAL.
Advantage: Faster development and testing.
Disadvantage: Slower execution compared to compiled languages.
Compiled Languages: These use a compiler to translate the entire program into machine language before execution. Examples include FORTRAN, COBOL, C.
Advantage: Faster execution.
Disadvantage: Takes more time to compile before running.

ADVANTAGES OF HIGH-LEVEL LANGUAGES (HLL) OVER MACHINE LANGUAGE (ML) AND LOW-LEVEL LANGUAGES (LLL)
Readability and Ease of Use:
HLL: Easy to read and write, resembling natural languages.
ML & LLL: Harder to understand, requiring hardware knowledge.
Portability:
HLL: Portable across different hardware with little modification.
ML & LLL: Machine-dependent, requiring rewriting for different hardware.
Development Speed:
HLL: Faster development due to built-in libraries and abstractions.
ML & LLL: Slower, as detailed hardware control is needed.
Maintainability:
HLL: Easier to maintain and modify with readable structure and tools.
ML & LLL: More difficult to maintain due to complex syntax.
Error Detection and Debugging:
HLL: Advanced tools for error detection and debugging.
ML & LLL: Debugging is harder due to proximity to machine instructions.
