INTERNET
Disadvantages of the internet
Privacy Risks:  Potential for data breaches and identity theft.
Cyberbullying: Online harassment and negative behavior.
Misinformation: Spread of false information and fake news.
Addiction: Excessive use leading to reduced productivity.
Security Threats: Vulnerability to viruses and hacking
Inappropriate Content: The internet can expose users to explicit, violent, and harmful material.
Social Isolation: Excessive online activity can lead to reduced face-to-face interactions and social isolation.
What is a Search Engine?
A search engine is a tool that helps you find information on the internet by indexing web pages and returning results based on your search keywords.
Examples of Search Engines
Google [www.google.com]: The most popular search engine worldwide.
Bing [www.bing.com]: Microsoft alternative to Google.
Yahoo [www.yahoo.com]: Offers search along with news and email services.
DuckDuckGo [www.duckduckgo.com]: Focuses on privacy and doesn’t track users.
Baidu [www.baidu.com]: Dominates the search market in China.
Yandex [www.yandex.com]: Popular in Russia, with localized services.

INTERNET
How to Use a Search Engine
Open a Browser: Use Chrome, Firefox, Safari, etc.
Go to the Search Engine: Type the URL (e.g., www.google.com).
Enter Keywords: Type in what you’re looking for (e.g., “benefits of internet”).
Review Results: Browse the list of links provided.
Click on a Result: Open a link that seems relevant.
Tips for Effective Searches
Use specific keywords for better accuracy.
Try different phrases if you don’t find what you need.
Use quotation marks for exact phrases (e.g., “best study tips”).
