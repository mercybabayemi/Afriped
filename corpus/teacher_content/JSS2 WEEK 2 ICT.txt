CLASSIFICATIONS OF COMPUTERS
CLASSIFICATION BASED ON GENERATION
Computers are also classified based on the generations of technological advancements they represent. Each generation marks a significant leap in computing technology, often characterized by changes in hardware and software.
First Generation (1940s - 1950s)
Technology: Vacuum Tubes
Examples: ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer), EDVAC (Electronic Discrete Variable Automatic Computer), EDSAC (electronic delay storage automatic calculator), UNIVAC (Universal Automatic Computer).
Characteristics:
Computers used vacuum tubes for circuitry and magnetic drums for memory.
These computers were enormous, occupying entire rooms.
Programming was done in machine language, which is the most basic form of language that the computer can understand directly.
Second Generation (1950s - 1960s)
Technology: Transistors
Examples: IBM 7090, UNIVAC II, ATLAS
Characteristics:
Transistors replaced vacuum tubes, making computers smaller, faster, more reliable, and more energy-efficient.
Magnetic cores were used as the primary memory, and magnetic tapes and disks were used for secondary storage.
High-level programming languages like FORTRAN and COBOL were developed.
Third Generation (1960s - 1970s)
Technology: Integrated Circuits (ICs)
Examples: IBM 360 series, PDP-8.
Characteristics:
Integrated Circuits (ICs) combined multiple transistors and other components on a single silicon chip, further reducing the size and cost of computers.
Computers became more powerful and were able to support multiple users simultaneously through time-sharing.
Keyboards and monitors began replacing punch cards and printouts as user interfaces.

CLASSIFICATION OF COMPUTERS
CLASSIFICATION BASED ON GENERATION
Fourth Generation (1970s - Present)
Technology: Microprocessors
Examples: Intel 4004 (first microprocessor), Apple Macintosh, IBM PC.
Characteristics:
The invention of the microprocessor (a single chip that could execute all the functions of a CPU) marked the beginning of the fourth generation.
Computers became even smaller, more powerful, and more affordable, leading to the proliferation of personal computers (PCs).
Graphical User Interfaces (GUIs), the mouse, and handheld devices like smartphones emerged.
Networking and the development of the Internet became widespread during this period.
Fifth Generation (Present and Beyond)
Technology: Artificial Intelligence (AI)
Examples: IBM Watson (AI), Google Quantum Computer.
Characteristics:
The fifth generation is characterized by advancements in AI, machine learning, and quantum computing.
These computers can process natural language, recognize patterns, and solve complex problems much faster than traditional computers.
Perform tasks using natural human interaction (voice recognition, natural language processing).
Perform complex calculations at unprecedented speeds.
