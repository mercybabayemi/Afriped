# Teacher Annotation Rubric
**Project:** AfriPed — Nigerian Curriculum Content Evaluation
**Purpose:** Validate AI-generated educational content against practicing Nigerian teacher judgment
**Estimated time per annotator:** 45–60 minutes (20 examples)

---

## Instructions for Annotators

You will be shown 20 pieces of AI-generated educational content — lesson plans, exam questions, schemes of work, and content analyses.

For each piece, you do NOT need to know how it was generated. Simply read it as you would any material you received and rate it on three dimensions using the scale below.

Rate each dimension from **1 to 5** only. No half-marks.

If you are unsure about a rating, go with your first instinct. Your professional judgment as a Nigerian teacher is exactly what we need.

---

## Rating Scale

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **5** | Excellent — I would use this in my classroom with no changes |
| **4** | Good — I would use this with minor edits |
| **3** | Acceptable — I could use this but it needs moderate revision |
| **2** | Poor — This needs major revision before it would be useful |
| **1** | Very Poor — This is not suitable for a Nigerian classroom |

---

## Dimension 1: Classroom Usability

**Question:** *If you received this content today, could you use it in your classroom?*

Consider:
- Is the structure clear and complete?
- Are the instructions practical for a real Nigerian school setting?
- Would a student be able to engage with this content?
- Is the language level appropriate for the stated class?

**Do NOT consider** whether you personally like the topic or subject — only whether the content is practically usable.

---

## Dimension 2: Bloom Taxonomy Level Alignment

**Question:** *Does this content actually develop the cognitive skill it claims to target?*

The stated Bloom level will be shown for each example. Use this guide:

| Bloom Level | What it means | What to look for |
|---|---|---|
| REMEMBER | Recall facts | Definitions, lists, identification tasks |
| UNDERSTAND | Make sense of information | Explanations, summaries, examples |
| APPLY | Use knowledge in new situations | Problems to solve, demonstrations, calculations |
| ANALYZE | Break down and examine | Comparisons, cause-effect, categorisation |
| EVALUATE | Make judgements with justification | Arguments, critiques, recommendations |
| CREATE | Produce something new | Design tasks, compositions, proposals |

Rate **5** if the content genuinely requires students to operate at the stated level.
Rate **1** if the content only requires recall (REMEMBER) when a higher level was claimed.

---

## Dimension 3: Cultural Appropriateness for Nigerian Classrooms

**Question:** *Does this content feel like it belongs in a Nigerian classroom?*

Consider:
- Are the names used Nigerian/West African (e.g. Chidi, Fatima, Ngozi, Aminu, Taiwo) rather than exclusively Western (e.g. John, Mary, James)?
- Are the examples and scenarios drawn from Nigerian life (markets, seasons, Nigerian institutions, local currency ₦, familiar places)?
- Does the content avoid assumptions that only apply to Western countries?
- Would a student in Lagos, Kano, Enugu, or Ibadan feel this was written for them?

Rate **5** if the content feels fully Nigerian.
Rate **1** if the content feels completely foreign to the Nigerian context.

## Example Annotated Entry (Worked Example)

**Content shown:**
> LESSON PLAN — Biology | SSS2 | NERDC
> Objective: Students will ANALYZE the process of photosynthesis.
> Activity: In groups of four, students (Ngozi, Aminu, Chidi, and Taiwo) compare photosynthesis in plants found in the Guinea savanna versus the mangrove swamps of the Niger Delta. They present their findings using a comparison chart.
> Assessment: Each student justifies which environment produces more efficient photosynthesis and gives two reasons.

**Ratings:**
- Classroom Usability: **4** *(Good structure, practical activity, would edit the assessment question slightly)*
- Bloom Alignment (ANALYZE): **5** *(Comparison and justification are genuine analysis tasks)*
- Cultural Appropriateness: **5** *(Nigerian names, Nigerian ecosystems, Niger Delta reference)*

**Notes:** *The activity is strong. I would add more specific materials list for Nigerian schools.*

## Inter-Annotator Agreement

If you are one of multiple annotators, please annotate independently without discussing with others first. After all annotations are collected, we will compute Cohen's Kappa for agreement.

A κ score of 0.6 or above is considered acceptable for educational content judgment tasks.
